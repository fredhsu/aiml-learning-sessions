## Momentum

Momentum improves gradient descent when the optimization space is ill conditioned, (has a large conditioning number). It does this by adding a decaying history of previous states to the update calculation. This cancels out the effect of sign flipping across the narrow ravine of an ill conditioned graph, accumulates in the direction of the shallow valley, and finds the optimal method of shrinking the error across all eigenvalues at once, improving convergence from O(kappa) go O(sqrt(kappa)).

## Three hyperparameter rules

1. Start beta = 0.9
2. Adjust learning rate first, then momentum
3. Higher $\kappa$ requires smaller $\beta$ to avoid sign flip accumulation

## Chebyshev and Eigendecomposition

Chebyshev/eigendecomposition give two different perspectives on how momentum improves the optimization algorithm. Eigendecomposition breaks down how the components contribute to cancelling sign flips and accumulating the consistent components. The heavy-ball recurrence implicitly generates the Chebyshev polynomial recurrence, which is optimal for shrinking error on $[λmin,λmax]$. This is only optimal for the quadratics with exact gradients. In practice this weakens as we deal with non-convex landscapes and stochastic gradients.
